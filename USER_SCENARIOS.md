### Scenario 1: The Relocating Student ðŸŽ“
**Situation:** A college student needs to dispose of an old laptop before moving to a new city.
**Problem:** They are busy, stressed, and financially motivated but don't know where to go or if the device has value.
**Punarchakra Solution:**
1.  **Value Estimation:** The student uses the **Scan Feature** at home. The AI identifies it as "Laptop (Old)" and estimates a value of **+500 Credits ($5 voucher)**. This financial incentive motivates them to recycle rather than trash it.
2.  **Location Finding:** They open the **Bin Map**. The map uses their GPS to show the *closest* active bin.
3.  **Route Guidance:** Clicking "Navigate" opens Google Maps with the optimal route, saving them time during their busy move.

### Scenario 3: The Ambiguous Charger ðŸ”Œ
**Situation:** A user deposits a phone charger, but the system is only 60% sure it's a cable.
**Problem:** AI uncertainty can lead to incorrect points or rejected valid items.
**Punarchakra Solution:**
1.  **Transparent Ambiguity:** The UI displays: *"Potential Match: Cable/Charger (Confidence: Neutral)"*.
2.  **User Verification:** It asks a simple "Yes/No" question: *"Is this an electrical cable?"*
3.  **Adaptive Learning:** If the user confirms "Yes," the item is accepted, and this data point is flagged for the system to learn from, improving accuracy for the next charger. The user feels trusted and part of the process.

### Scenario 4: The Full Bin Redirect ðŸš«
**Situation:** A user arrives at their usual bin, but it is at 100% capacity.
**Problem:** A wasted trip leads to frustration and potential littering (leaving the item Next to the bin).
**Punarchakra Solution:**
1.  **Pre-Emptive Alerts:** Before they even leave home, the **Map** shows that bin as a **Red Pin (Full)**.
2.  **Automatic Redirection:** If they click it, the app says, *"This bin is full. The nearest available bin is 400m away at [Location]."*
3.  **Incentive to Travel:** The app could offer a "Bonus +20 Points" for traveling to the slightly further, non-full bin, turning the inconvenience into a gamified opportunity.

### Scenario 5: The Admin Route Planner ðŸš›
**Situation:** A fleet manager needs to plan tomorrow's collection route for 500 bins.
**Problem:** Collecting from empty bins wastes fuel; missing full bins causes overflow.
**Punarchakra Solution:**
1.  **Data-Driven Dashboard:** The Admin Dashboard displays a "Bins by Fill Level" chart.
2.  **Predictive Analytics:** It highlights not just currently full bins, but "Fast Filling" bins (e.g., a bin that went from 20% to 80% in 2 hours).
3.  **Optimized Instructions:** It generates a filtered list of only "Critical" and "Warning" status bins, effectively creating a focused "Pick-up List" for drivers, reducing fuel consumption and ensuring no overflows occur.
